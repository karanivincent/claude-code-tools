# TeliTask Brand Voice — Quick Reference

Full guidelines live at `Telitask/Marketing/brand/brand-voice-guidelines.md` (note its positioning banner — the pricing and audience sections there are out of date). This is the compressed version for use during page copywriting.

## The one-line positioning

TeliTask is **voice-first AI that makes the calls a business already makes every day** — delivery confirmations, check-in rounds with field or regional staff, follow-ups after a failed attempt. Always lead with the phone call itself, not the app or the dashboard.

**Positioning changed in July 2026.** The product was previously a personal AI executive assistant for individuals at $79/month. It is now aimed at businesses that employ people to make routine calls. Older copy elsewhere may still reflect the old framing — do not copy it.

## Anchoring

- **Anchor on the staff currently making these calls.** The comparison a prospect makes is "my people spend all morning on this."
- **Do not anchor against human VAs / executive assistants** ($3,000/month). That framing belonged to the personal-assistant product.
- **Never anchor against other AI tools** (ChatGPT, Notion AI, etc.). Wrong reference point.
- **Never state a price.** Pricing for this direction is unset. Leave figures off the page entirely.

## What to lead with

- **Lead with the calls they make.** "Three hundred confirmation calls before the day even starts."
- **Do not lead with calls they're missing.** The after-hours / missed-call angle was rejected by three of three discovery prospects. Use it only if this prospect raised it themselves. Evidence: `Telitask/Marketing/strategy/discovery-call-log.md`.
- **Pre-empt "pre-recorded robot."** Prospects assume an automated line means an IVR menu. The fastest cure is getting them to tap a scenario and hear a real conversation, so keep copy short and the call obvious.

## Banned words / phrases

These signal generic SaaS copy and break the voice:

- "automate" / "automation" → use **"handles"**, **"makes the calls"**, **"takes care of"**
- "notification" / "alert" → use **"phone call"**, **"calls you"**
- "workflow" → use **"the round of calls"**, **"things get done"**
- "chatbot" → never; use **"AI assistant"** or **"voice assistant"**
- "leverage" → use **"use"**
- "revolutionary" / "cutting-edge" / "next-gen" → cut entirely, don't replace
- "seamless" / "seamlessly" → cut entirely
- "empower" / "empowering" → cut entirely
- "robust" / "scalable" / "enterprise-grade" → cut, these are marketing filler

## Preferred phrases

- "Makes the calls your team makes every morning"
- "Voice-first"
- "Proactive"
- "AI assistant" / "voice assistant"
- "Nobody on your team has to make them"
- "Things get done"

## Tone

- Sharp, authentic, founder voice
- Direct and specific over corporate and broad
- A real person wrote this — not a marketing team
- Confident but not salesy
- No emojis in copy

## Examples

**Bad:** "TeliTask leverages AI to automate your call workflow with seamless follow-ups."
**Good:** "The confirmation calls still get made every morning. Nobody on your team has to make them."

**Bad:** "Never miss another customer call after hours."
**Good:** "Every failed delivery gets a callback the same day, without anyone picking up a phone."

**Bad:** "Like a human assistant for $79/month."
**Good:** "Ten people on the phones every morning. This is what one of those calls sounds like without them."

## Specific to founder_note copy

The founder note is from **Vincent**, sole founder, based in Kenya. It should sound like a real DM, not a marketing email.

- Reference something specific — best of all, something the prospect actually said in conversation
- Use first person, present tense
- Short sentences
- One concrete reason this is for them, not a generic pitch
- Tell them plainly that tapping a scenario will call them
- No statistics, no price
- Sign-off optional — usually no sign-off, since their face/name is right there in the UI

## Specific to pain_points copy

- Frame in the prospect's voice, not TeliTask's voice
- Short single-line bullets, sentence case
- No trailing punctuation
- Outcomes they want, not features TeliTask has
- A real number they gave you is the strongest line available

**Bad:** "We help you automate driver check-ins."
**Good:** "Knowing every driver actually started their route on time"
