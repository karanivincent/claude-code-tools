# TeliTask Brand Voice — Quick Reference

Full guidelines live at `Telitask/Marketing/brand/brand-voice-guidelines.md`. This is the compressed version for use during page copywriting.

## The one-line positioning

TeliTask is a **voice-first AI executive assistant that calls you on the phone**. Always lead with the phone call — not the app, not the dashboard, not notifications.

## Anchoring

- **Always anchor against human VAs** ($3,000+/month). TeliTask is $79/month and delivers the same outcomes.
- **Never anchor against other AI tools** (ChatGPT, Notion AI, etc.). Wrong reference point.

## Banned words / phrases

These signal generic SaaS copy and break the voice:

- "automate" / "automation" → use **"handles"**, **"calls you"**, **"takes care of"**
- "notification" / "alert" → use **"phone call"**, **"calls you"**
- "workflow" → use **"things get done"**, **"the work happens"**
- "chatbot" → never; use **"AI assistant"** or **"voice assistant"**
- "leverage" → use **"use"**
- "revolutionary" / "cutting-edge" / "next-gen" → cut entirely, don't replace
- "seamless" / "seamlessly" → cut entirely
- "empower" / "empowering" → cut entirely
- "robust" / "scalable" / "enterprise-grade" → cut, these are marketing filler

## Preferred phrases

- "Calls you on the phone"
- "Voice-first"
- "Proactive" (vs. reactive notifications)
- "AI assistant" / "voice assistant"
- "Like having a personal VA, for $79/month"
- "Things get done"

## Tone

- Sharp, authentic, founder voice
- Direct and specific over corporate and broad
- A real person wrote this — not a marketing team
- Confident but not salesy
- No emojis in copy

## Examples

**Bad:** "TeliTask leverages AI to automate your workflow with seamless notifications."
**Good:** "TeliTask calls you when something needs attention — not another notification to ignore."

**Bad:** "Empower your team with cutting-edge productivity automation."
**Good:** "Your AI assistant handles the follow-ups, reminders, and check-in calls. For $79/month."

**Bad:** "Streamline your operations with our robust voice platform."
**Good:** "Like having a personal VA who actually calls people — for the cost of one lunch a week."

## Specific to founder_note copy

The founder note is from **Vincent**, sole founder, based in Kenya. It should sound like a real DM, not a marketing email.

- Reference something specific from research (proves you looked)
- Use first person, present tense
- Short sentences
- One concrete reason this is for them, not a generic pitch
- Sign-off optional — usually no sign-off, since their face/name is right there in the UI

## Specific to pain_points copy

- Frame in the prospect's voice, not TeliTask's voice
- Short single-line bullets, sentence case
- No trailing punctuation
- Outcomes they want, not features TeliTask has

**Bad:** "We help you automate driver check-ins."
**Good:** "Knowing every driver actually started their route on time"
