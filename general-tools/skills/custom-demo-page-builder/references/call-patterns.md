# Call Patterns (operator-facing)

A menu, not a template. Pick what fits the prospect in front of you. Everything here
briefs **you** while you build the page — **none of it goes on the prospect-facing
page**, least of all the numbers.

## How to use this file

Work out which calls this business depends on, then find the closest shapes below.
Most businesses have both directions; few have all of them. If nothing fits, reason
from first principles: who rings them, who they ring, and what breaks when a call
doesn't happen.

## Outbound: calls the business makes

Repeated, structured, usually somebody's whole job.

| Pattern | What it is | Who receives it |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-work confirmation | Confirming details before anything ships, dispatches, or starts — location, time window, who'll receive it | Customer |
| Retry after a failed attempt | The delivery or visit didn't land; find out what happened and rebook | Customer |
| Status round with own staff | Ringing each driver, field agent, or regional rep to collect the day and flag escalations | Staff |
| Appointment reminder | Reducing no-shows the day before | Customer |
| Payment follow-up | Chasing an overdue balance | Customer |
| Supplier check | Confirming stock, availability, or a delivery window | Supplier |

The tell that this matters: **people are employed to make these calls.** Job listings
for customer care, dispatch, tele-sales, or a call centre are the clearest public
signal. Branches, fleets, and field staff imply a status round.

## Inbound: calls the business takes

| Pattern | What it is |
|---|---|
| New-customer capture | Someone rings wanting to buy or book |
| Urgent triage | An emergency that needs escalating or slotting in now |
| Rebooking / reschedule | Moving an existing appointment |
| Missed-call callback | A call came in unanswered; the business rings back |

### What discovery has shown about the inbound framing

The after-hours missed-call angle was TeliTask's original wedge and has been
**rejected by three of three prospects** who were asked directly. Two rejected it
emphatically: evening cover turns out to be a solved problem for anyone large enough
to feel it — shift workers, a customer-relations person, or an owner who simply picks
up at midnight.

That is not a ban. It means: don't lead with it unless *this* prospect raised it
themselves. If they did, it's a fine scenario. Evidence and full notes:
`Telitask/Marketing/strategy/discovery-call-log.md` — read it before leaning on any
framing.

### Inbound stats (operator context only)

Useful for sizing a prospect's exposure when inbound genuinely is their problem.
Never put these on a page.

- 40–50% of inbound calls arrive outside standard 9-to-5 hours.
- ~62% of small-business inbound calls go unanswered.
- 80–86% of callers who hit voicemail hang up and call the next business instead.

Source: `Telitask/telitask-development/docs/reports/off-hours-call-businesses-research.md`.

## Per-industry starting points

Where the calls tend to sit. Use as a prompt for your own thinking, not as an answer.

| Industry | Inbound that matters | Outbound that matters |
|---|---|---|
| Distribution / FMCG | Order enquiries | Delivery confirmation rounds, failed-drop retries, regional rep status calls |
| Logistics / courier | Where-is-my-parcel | Pre-delivery confirmation, retry after a failed attempt, driver check-ins |
| Manufacturing with field staff | Order and stock enquiries | Daily regional check-in round, escalation collection |
| Veterinary | Urgent triage; after-hours booking | Post-treatment follow-up, appointment reminders |
| Restaurant | Reservations, large parties, catering | Booking confirmations |
| Home services | Emergency dispatch | Pre-visit confirmation, retry after a missed visit |
| Real estate | Listing enquiries | Viewing reminders, post-viewing follow-up |
| Dental / medical | Rebooking, new-patient enquiry | Appointment reminders, recall rounds |
| Hotel / lodging | Late check-in, last-minute booking | Pre-arrival confirmation |

## Kenya / WhatsApp note (internal only)

In Kenya many customers start on WhatsApp rather than a voice call (~85–97% of Kenyan
internet users are on WhatsApp daily). The TeliTask demo is always a voice call, so:

- Keep the page voice-only. Do not promise WhatsApp handling on the page.
- Be honest with the **user** (Vincent), not the prospect: for a WhatsApp-heavy
  consumer-facing business, a pure voice demo may underweight the channel where their
  customers actually start. Flag it when relevant so expectations are set before
  outreach. This matters far less for internal outbound calling — staff and drivers
  are reached by phone.
